A LOVE FOR TECHNOLOGY

We live in an exciting time in history. For decades, technology has been swiftly empowering humanity towards a brighter future. From gaming to medicine, communication to space exploration, technology is pushing us further than we ever thought possible. Even as a kid, I knew that I wanted to be a part of this movement.

My degree, personal projects, past work experience, and ongoing professional development have all been geared towards a better understanding of how technology can enhance our lives. My skill set includes a knowledge of both hardware and software, Windows, Linux, and OSX operating systems, and a variety of programming languages, DBMS's, and run time environments such as: Java, Javascript, JQuery, Node.js, Pug, HTML, CSS, MySQL/Oracle, Mongoose/MongoDB,  C/C++ (arduino subset), and C# 

I have experience in building complex, modular applications using many different design patterns, API’s, and many testing methods to ensure a high level of accuracy in code for both functional and nonfunctional requirements. My professional software development and testing experience includes my employment at ICE Health Systems, where I was given opportunities to expand my knowledge and skills to an industry leading standard. While working at ICE, I gained experience quality assurance through the implementation of end to end automated tests using many industry leading testing suites like JUnit, Selenium with BrowserStack, and Puppeteer.

My work and volunteering experience demonstrates my passion for people and my strong interpersonal and communication skills. Most recently, I was nominated as an ambassador for the second year Computer Information System students at Mount Royal University, where I had the privilege of actively contributing to the formal review process of the BCIS program. I also strive to share my passion for technology with others, as demonstrated by my recent volunteer work with the Explore IT event, where I shared my knowledge and experience in object-oriented programming and the BCIS program with prospective female MRU students.
